Text:

This line forms the direct route to Doncaster. Retracing our steps by the Sheffield and Rotherham Branch, and passing [Masborough](/stations/masborough) station. The next in our course is

(place: england/yorkshire/rawmarsh)

[Kilnhurst](/stations/kilnhurst) station.

(place: england/yorkshire/swinton)

[Mexborough Junction](/stations/mexborough-junction).

(branch: start)
  [Conisborough](/stations/conisborough).--Here is a castle belonging to the Duke of Leeds, built at the time of the Conquest, with a keep 78 feet high, in a good state of preservation.

  [Sprotborough](/stations/sprotborough).--In the vicinity is the Hall, seat of Sir J. Copley, Bart., built in the 17th century, and Cusworth Park, a beautiful place.

  (place: england/yorkshire/doncaster)
(branch: end)

The next three stations, [Wath](/stations/wath-north), [Darfield](/stations/darfield), and [Cudworth](/stations/cudworth), for Barnsley, have no particular interest.

[Royston and Notton](/stations/royston-and-notton).--In the vicinity are *Chevet Hall*, Lady Pilkington; and *Walton Park*, with its menagerie and collection, the seat of C. Waterton, Esq., the South American traveller.

[Oakenshaw](/stations/oakenshaw).--In the vicinity is *Heath Hall*, seat of J. Smythe, Esq.

(place: england/yorkshire/normanton)

[Methley](/stations/methley).--Here is an ancient church, with the Savile's tombs. In the vicinity are *Kippax Hall*, seat of T. Bland, Esq., a noble range, partly Elizabethan; *Ledstone Hall*, H. Ramsden, Esq.; and *Methley Park*, Lord Mexborough's.

[Woodlesford](/stations/woodlesford).--In the vicinity are *Templenewsam House*, seat of H. Ingram, Esq., with fine collection of paintings and gems of *vertu*. Here Darnley, Mary Queen of Scots' husband, was born, when it belonged to the Lennoxes. The battlements round the roof make this inscription--"All glory and praise be given to God the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost on high. Peace on earth, goodwill to man. Honour and true allegiance to our gracious king; loving affection among his subjects; health and plenty within this house." *Swillington*, Sir J. Lowther, Bart., near the old Gothic Church. *Harewood*, Earl of Harewood. *Farnley Park*, W. Armitage, Esq.

[Hunslet](/stations/hunslet) station.

(place: england/yorkshire/leeds)
