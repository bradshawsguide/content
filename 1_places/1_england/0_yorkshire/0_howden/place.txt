Text: (smcp: Howden) has a fine decorated church, with the beautiful east window and chapter house of a college of the 13th century, belonging to the Bishops of Durham, and founded in 1266; it contains a fine tower 140 feet high, east window, and handsome octagon chapel house. The Bishop's Palace is now a farm-house. Close by is *Spaldington Grange*, the seat of Lord Howden. Roger de Howden, a monkish chronicler, was a native. A great horse fair is held here for hunters, cavalry horses, &c.
