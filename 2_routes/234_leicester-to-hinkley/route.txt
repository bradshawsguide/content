(place: england/leicestershire/leicester)

Passengers by this line book at Leicester, the station lying on the east side of the town. Leaving the gaol to our right and the race-course to our left, we pass under the road to Wigston, and through it short tunnel to Wigston Junction, about two miles south of Leicester, and the point of deviation of the above line. The first station is

[Blaby](/stations/blaby), one mile to the north of which is *Aylestone*, with the ivy patch on its church spire, the ancient seat of the Vernons and of the Earls of Rutland, now occupied by N. C. Stone, Esq., the Duke of Rutland's agent.
