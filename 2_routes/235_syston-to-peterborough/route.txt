(place: england/leicestershire/syston-junction)

[Rearsby](/stations/rearsby) station.

(place: england/leicestershire/brooksby)

[Frisby](/stations/frisby) and [Ashfordby](/stations/asfordby) stations follow next in succession, in the vicinity of the latter of which is *Kirby Park*, formerly the seat of Sir Francis Burdett. Here he wrote his celebrated Reform Letter, which caused his committal to the Tower of London.

(place: england/leicestershire/melton-mowbray)

**Saxby**.--In the vicinity is *Stapleford Park*; the old seat of the Earl of Harborough.

[Whisendine](/stations/whisendine), the entrance to the county of Rutland, and [Ashwell](/stations/ashwell) stations.

(place: england/rutland/oakham)

[Manton](/stations/manton) station, for *Uppingham*, at which place there is one of Archdeacon Johnson's schools, and a church of which Jeremy Taylor was once rector.

[Luffenham](/stations/luffenham) station.

(place: england/rutland/ketton)

(place: england/lincolnshire/stamford)

Passing [Uffington](/stations/uffington-and-barnack), [Helpstone](/stations/helpstone), and [Walton](/stations/walton) stations, we arrive at the Crescent Station.

(place: england/northamptonshire/peterborough)
