[Ashchurch](/stations/ashchurch-for-tewkesbury), from whence a short branch to our right brings us to

Leaving Tewkesbury, we immediately cross the Avon, and soon arrive at [Ripple](/stations/ripple), 3½ miles, soon after which we approach the Severn, along the margin of which we travel a short distance to the town of

(place: england/worcestershire/upton-on-severn)

A neatly-built town with a considerable trade, the river being navigable here for vessels of 100 tons burthen. A Roman road runs between the two towns of Upton and Tewkesbury. Dr. Lee, the astrologer was born here. A distance of 4½ miles further brings the traveller to [Malvern Wells](/stations/malvern-wells), and 2¼ more to
