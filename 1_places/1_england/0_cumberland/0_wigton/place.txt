text: A market, town situated on the Wyre, with a population of 714, employed in the cotton and print trade. It contains town hall, free school, print and cotton factories, four chapels, and a church, rebuilt in 1746. In, the river is good trout and chub fishing; close by are the remains of *Greenhalgh Castle*, which was garrisoned by the Earl of Derby for Charles I. in 1643. The Pretender occupied this place in 1715. In the vicinity are *Kirkland Hall* seat of T. Cole, Esq.; *Bleasdale Tower*, seat of W. J. Garnett, Esq., and *Bleasdale Fell*, 1,709 feet high.
