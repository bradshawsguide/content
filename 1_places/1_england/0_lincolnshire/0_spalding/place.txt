Text: This is a market town, with a population of 7,032, and capital of the district termed Hollands. It contains five Chapels, Town Hall (by Hobson, a native), Theatre, Literary Club, Assembly Rooms, Market House, &c. Its church is of the 15th century, and has a good porch and a crocketed spire. Roman cisterns have been found here. Bentley was master of the grammar school. Johnson, the antiquarian, was a native. In the vicinity is a carious old moated seat at Pinchbeck.
