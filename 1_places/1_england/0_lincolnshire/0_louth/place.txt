Text: A large municipal town, with a population of 10,560. It contains a beautiful Gothic church, with a spire 288 feet high. In the cemetery formerly stood St. Mary's Church, the vicarage of which was converted into a hermitage The Corn Exchange is a fine edifice. Edward VI. founded the grammar school; its income amounts to Â£1,463, and the seal "a boy birched by his master," with the pithy motto, "Qui parcit verge," &c., ("Whoso spareth the rod hateth the child.")--Sharp's *British Gazetteer*. In the vicinity are *Yarborough*, the old seat of the Yarburgh family, *Cockerington House*, W. Scrope, Esq., descendant of the ancient family of that name in Richard II.'s time; and the decayed port of Saltfleet.
