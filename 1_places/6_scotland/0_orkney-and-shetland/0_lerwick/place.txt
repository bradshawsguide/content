Title: Lerwick

----

Section: 3

----

Text:

Is visited weekly by the mail steamer from Granton, calling at Aberdeen, Wick, and Kirkwall, on its passage. The town is not devoid of attraction, but the islands are especially interesting. The steamer leaves on its return voyage every Monday evening.

Shetland has, as well as Orkney, been made famous by the novels of Sir Walter Scott, whence many of his leading sea characters were obtained. The headlands of Sumburgh and others are of prodigious height, and one of the most exciting places to visit is the cradle of Noss. The lighthouse at Unst, built by Messrs. Stevenson, C.E., is a wonderful proof of human energy and talent
