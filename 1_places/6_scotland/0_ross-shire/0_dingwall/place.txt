text: The county town of Ross, and a parliamentary borough, prettily situated about 5 miles to the south of Ben Wynnis and at the south west corner of the Cromarty Firth. This splendid estuary is 12 miles long, and 2 miles broad, and forms a magnificent harbour and place ot refuge for vesssls in stormy weather. The town carries on a trade with Edinburgh, and even London. It has a pillar, 57 feet high, erected to the memory of one of the Earls of Cromarty.
